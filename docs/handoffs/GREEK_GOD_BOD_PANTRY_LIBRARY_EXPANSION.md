## PROFILE LOCK

Greek God Bod cooking philosophy distilled from Luke's 20-recipe canon:
- **Lean meats first.** Bison, eye of round, flank, lean ground turkey, lean pork, venison, swordfish, shrimp.
- **Vegetables as base.** Spaghetti squash, zucchini, cauliflower rice, arugula, cabbage, mushrooms.
- **Tomato + herb + spice anchors.** Crushed tomatoes, capers, olives, garlic, basil, cumin, gochujang.
- **Greek yogurt as utility.** Sauce base, marinade, garnish.
- **Mediterranean / Mexican / Greek influence.** Olive oil controlled, lemon abundant, citrus-forward.
- **Avoid:** heavy cream sauces, fatty cuts, breaded fried things, sugar-bomb desserts.

Existing 33 products in the library overlap minimally with this list — current library leans toward breakfast staples (eggs, oats, cottage cheese, milks, fruits) plus convenience items (Yasso, Magic Spoon, REBBL). Greek God Bod expansion is mostly additive, not duplicative.

## DOCTRINE CARRY-FORWARDS FOR LUKE

Worth re-reading before the bulk-add session:

1. **Existing products skipped via dedup.** Anything that matches the existing 33 (Bananas, Eggs - Large, Magic Spoon, Yasso, etc.) will surface "Already in library" badges. Don't re-add.

2. **Generic foods → prefer USDA, not OFF.** Per Gamma B F.1, OFF can mismatch generics. For "Broccoli florets" / "Chicken breast" / "Brown rice" — USDA Foundation/FNDDS hits are most accurate.

3. **Branded foods → OFF or USDA-Branded.** For "Fage 0% Greek yogurt" or "Siggi's plain skyr" — OFF is usually best.

4. **Recipe anchors → LLM-fill manual macros.** Bison bolognese, beef pho, etc. won't have OFF/USDA matches. Use LLM-fill mode and enter your own macro estimates per serving. This is fine — they're your own composites.

5. **Confidence labels visible.** OFF nutriscore-curated entries get 'high'; USDA Foundation/FNDDS gets 'high'; everything else 'medium' or 'low'. Prefer 'high' when picking.

6. **Take breaks.** 247 entries is real cognitive load. Three 30-min sessions beats one 90-min slog.

7. **Empirical post-add measurement.** After the bulk-add, voice-log a meal containing 3-4 of the newly-added foods. Library shortcut hit rate should jump from current ~10% to potentially 40-60% on meals composed of bulk-added items.
